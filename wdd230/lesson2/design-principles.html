Visual Hierarchy

KSL

ksl.com

The KSL news site exemplifies visual hierarchy. The eyes are first drawn to a large headline story about Donald Trump. Secondary articles that are significantly smaller sit below the headline article. Finally, even articles with even smaller images appear at the bottom.

Hick's Law

Dogpile

dogpile.com

Dogpile does an excellent job at conveying Hick's Law. The site is very minimal. All of the focus is on the search bar, with the exception of a single link for coronavirus information. As a user, it is easy to focus on the main purpose of the website: To search the Internet.

Law of Symmetry

Microsoft Azure Portal

portal.azure.com

The Microsoft Azure portal uses symmetry to help the user make sense of a lot of information! It groups related pieces of information together. Some information flows vertically, and other information flows horizontally. Either way, it provides the user an easy way to understand various details about their cloud resources.
